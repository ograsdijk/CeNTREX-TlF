# Work log — 2026-05-05

Found and fixed a real bug in the simulator's initial-state preparation
(`adiabatic_dressed_initial_states`) that was producing the **wrong dressed
state** as Psi0 for the actual Ramsey simulation. After the fix, the
far-frequency fringe scan with `phi2 = +π/2` lands the central crossing at
exactly **F_RES = 119.514 kHz with survival = 0.4909 ≈ 0.5**, matching the
analytical Ramsey expectation and the user's prior-knowledge estimate.

The earlier "fringe centerline ~110 Hz off F_RES" perception was a real
symptom of this bug (Psi0 was the *wrong* polarized branch — wrong-mJ in
some Jmax cases — and its time evolution gave a fringe pattern unrelated to
the intended Tl-spin resonance), not a phase-convention artifact as I
incorrectly diagnosed across the prior several rounds.

## Why the resonance frequency was wrong

`F_RES` was originally hardcoded as **119.640 kHz**, which was the eigenvalue
splitting at **Jmax=3** (small basis, only J ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3} represented). The
converged value at Jmax ≥ 4 is **119.514 kHz** — about 130 Hz lower. Two
compounding issues hid this:

### Issue 1 — basis-size truncation

At Jmax=3 the bare-J=2 weight at 30 kV/cm is captured but bare J=3+ is not.
The polarized branch's energy at high field comes from second-order Stark
mixing with all higher J states; truncating at J=3 over-estimates the energy
gap to the partner by ~130 Hz. By Jmax=5–7 the splitting is stable to 0.3 Hz.

### Issue 2 — wrong dressed state identified at high field

When the simulator calls `adiabatic_dressed_initial_states(... E_init=2 kV/cm,
E_target=30 kV/cm ...)` it needs to produce Psi0 (the dressed state at the
trajectory's start, low E) that, when adiabatically transported to 30 kV/cm
inside the Ramsey region, becomes the polarized branch the user wants to drive
(the one mostly characterized by bare |J=1, mJ=−1, m_Tl=−1/2, m_F=−1/2⟩ at
high E).

The OLD algorithm walked **HIGH → LOW**:

1. Diagonalize H at E_target = 30 kV/cm.
2. Pick the eigenvector with max overlap to the bare target (`find_closest_vector_idx(bare, V_high)`).
3. Walk down to E_init = 2 kV/cm, tracking by max overlap step-to-step.
4. Return the resulting eigenvector as Psi0.

Step 2 is unreliable for two compounding reasons:

(a) **The polarized branch isn't J=1-dominant at high field.** At 30 kV/cm
with Jmax=6, the polarized branch's bare composition is roughly 60% J=1, 35%
J=2, 5% J=3. The bare-J=1 weight is spread across many polarized eigenstates
(different mJ projections, all heavily Stark-mixed), so picking by
"max overlap with bare J=1, mJ=−1, m_Tl=−1/2, m_F=−1/2" can land on a
neighboring polarized branch instead of the right one.

(b) **Time-reversal degeneracy at B=0.** The static Stark Hamiltonian is
invariant under (mJ → −mJ, m_Tl → −m_Tl, m_F → −m_F). Opposite-sign-m
branches are *exactly* degenerate. `numpy.linalg.eigh` returns arbitrary
linear combinations within degenerate subspaces, so the "right eigenstate"
might be returned as a 50/50 mix of the desired mJ=−1 branch and the
opposite mJ=+1 branch. Even when picked by overlap, the result is then
ill-defined.

Empirically, at Jmax=4–7 the OLD walk landed at high E on a state with ~57%
**bare J=1, mJ=+1, m_Tl=+1/2, m_F=+1/2** character (the *opposite*-mJ branch)
— then walking down gave a Psi0 that was the wrong polarized branch's
adiabatic ancestor. The simulator then propagated this wrong state through
the Ramsey sequence, and the far-frequency scan showed a fringe pattern
shifted from F_RES because it was scanning a *different* transition's
phase-roll than the one F_RES labels.

| Jmax | OLD vs NEW Psi0 fidelity |
|---|---|
| 3 | 0.998 (small basis, both algorithms accidentally agree) |
| 4 | 0.93 |
| 5 | **0.003** |
| 6 | **0.000** ← cached scans were a totally different state |
| 7 | 0.999 (degeneracy lifts here for non-physical numpy reasons) |

## Fix

Two changes in `ramsey_rf/states.py`:

1. **Always walk LOW → HIGH** in both `adiabatic_dressed_initial_states` and
   the new `adiabatic_branch_to_field` helper. At low E the dressed states
   are nearly bare (overlap ~0.99 to the bare quantum numbers), so picking
   by bare-overlap is unambiguous. The walk then transports the well-defined
   low-E branch up to high E.

2. **Add a tiny `walk_B_z = 1e-4 G` symmetry-breaking field during the walk.**
   This lifts the (m → −m) Kramers degeneracy via a ~0.12 Hz Zeeman shift
   on m_Tl, which is enough to separate the eigenvalues numerically without
   physically affecting the ~120 kHz transition. Psi0 itself is computed at
   the user-supplied B (default 0) so the simulator sees the right physical
   initial state; only the diagnostic walk uses the perturbed B.

Two parallel fixes:

3. **`reference.py:F_RES`** is now computed at module load via
   `_compute_F_RES`, which uses `adiabatic_branch_to_field` with two bare
   targets (the populated state and its Tl-spin partner) at E_anchor =
   E_LOW, E_final = E_HIGH. Result: 119.514 kHz at Jmax ≥ 4, stable to
   0.3 Hz across Jmax ≤ 7. (Previously hardcoded as 119.640 kHz from Jmax=3.)

4. **`_find_static_branch_resonance`** in
   `far_frequency_fringes_static_field.ipynb` was rewritten to use the same
   helper. The static-field notebook had previously reported 120.071 kHz
   (with a different brand of the same bug) — it now also gives 119.514 kHz.

## Lessons / not done

- The phase-convention story I clung to across multiple rounds was wrong.
  The simulator's `phi₂ = +π/2` does map directly to Δφ_RWA = +π/2 once Psi0
  is correct; the apparent miscalibration was the wrong-state propagation
  masquerading as a phase issue. **Should have audited Psi0 sooner**, and
  not over-trusted a calibration scan whose own foundation depended on the
  same buggy Psi0.
- The static-field notebook still depends on
  `build_reference_config(dc_profile="static", ...)` which is not in the
  current `reference.py` — that infrastructure needs to be added back, or
  the notebook needs to switch to a self-contained config builder.
- Velocity-averaged scan (~21 h) and resonance-investigation scans
  (~3-4 h combined) have not been regenerated yet.
